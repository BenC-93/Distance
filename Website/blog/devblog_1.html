Networking and Bug Hunting

Mar 01 2013Ben

A core feature of our game is that the players are separated from one another for a good portion of gameplay, in order to fight monsters and collect items. We call the portions when the players are apart divergence, and when they are together convergence. Because there is so little documentation for networking in Unreal, we’ve decided to adopt an alternate system: we will have three different play areas. Two of these areas will be identical and serve to remove the players from one another during divergence. The last area, used for convergence, is where both players will be teleported to in order to fight larger boss monsters together. Dylan and Brittany have made a ton of progress in setting up divergence and convergence. Dylan looked into teleporting world objects in Unreal, doing some tests with items and controlled players, and set up functions for starting and ending convergence. Brittany jumped into the level editor and began setting up different regions to use for the three areas previously mentioned. While setting up the three environments, Brittany met with the artists to talk about color palettes and tone, and some of the content they came up with is really amazing!

Although we aren’t taking a networking approach to separating and joining the players, there is still a lot of networking that needs to happen to get the game fully functional. The whole team spent a bunch of time researching networking for Unreal, in both C++ and Unreal’s native Blueprint system. I took the bulk of trying to implement the networking to small success, but I was able to successfully connect to machines and pass game information. I’m sure this will remain a huge and ongoing task as we need to implement more of the game’s core aspects.

On the brighter side, Jordan, with the help of our technical artist Joe, managed to get the character animated and into the game! As the character moves around, it animates using a model and rig that Joe set up in Blender. We still need to look into adding items to the player’s hand, but that will come soon enough. Jordan also did some research and testing with lighting and normal maps on the player, and boy does it look good! We are looking forward to adding those effects in-game soon, which will help convey the tone so much better.

Besides a pesky bug that ate up a bit of our time, we really got a lot done this week. We are moving on to more art stuff soon, so keep an eye out on the art section of our main page!
